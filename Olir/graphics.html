Graphic design is the process of visual communication and problem-solving through the use of typography, photography, and illustration. It encompasses various techniques and methods to create and combine words, symbols, and images to create visual representations of ideas and messages. It includes branding, typography, digital media design, advertising design, and packaging design, among others. Graphic designers use various tools and software, such as Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, to create and manipulate visual elements.

  • Advertising: We create visual concepts for print, television, and online ads to communicate ideas that inspire, inform, or captivate consumers.
  • Branding and Identity: We create visual identities for companies, products, and services. This includes creating logos, typography, color schemes, and other visual elements that are used consistently across all branding materials.
  • Digital Media: We create designs for websites, mobile apps, and other digital media. They may also create graphics, animations, and videos to be used on social media, online ads, and other digital platforms.
  • Packaging: We the visual packaging for products, such as food, cosmetics, and consumer electronics. They work to make the packaging eye-catching and informative, while also ensuring it meets legal requirements.
  • Print: We create designs for print materials such as brochures, posters, billboards, books, and magazines.
  • Signage and Environmental Design: We create visual graphics for public spaces such as billboards, signs, and murals. They also design visual elements for exhibitions and events.
  • Television and Film: We create the visual elements for title sequences, film posters, and other promotional materials.
  • User Interface and User Experience design: We use design principles and techniques to create the layout, navigation, and visual elements of websites, mobile apps, and other digital products. They also work to improve the user experience by making the products easy to use and understand.

We use hand-illustrated designs as well as computer-aided designs thanks to a wide range of software with nearly endless digital design tools. The availability of software like Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

Besides turning their client’s vision, brand image and value proposition into a graphic display, a designer will undertake many specialty tasks as part of a graphic design project. The specialty tasks include collaborating on the concept (usually with a team), attending meetings about the project, paying attention to what customers are clicking on, doing presentations that explain the various potential designs, revising designs, and preparing asset files for others on the team and for client use.
